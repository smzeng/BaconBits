General Info About African Swine Fever

African swine fever (ASF) is an infectious disease of swine notifiable in the European Union (EU) and to the World Organization for Animal Health (OIE). Susceptible pigs can be infected by direct or indirect contact with infectious animals or their fluids, ingestion of contaminated animal feed, pork, or pig products, or contact with contaminated surfaces or fomites (clothing, footwear, vehicles, farming tools, etc.) acting as mechanical vectors. In the southern and eastern parts of the African continent and the Iberian Peninsula, ASF can also be transmitted by biological vectors, infected soft ticks belonging to the Ornithodoros genus. No vaccine or treatment is available against ASF, so the mortality rate is extremely high.

Shower-In, Shower-Out

Through this biosecurity measure, employees and visitors are asked to remove footwear in the initial entryway of farm in order to gain access to farm. They are asked to proceed to "dirty" side of shower and take off all clothing.

People looking to enter the farm are required to thoroughly lshower and shampoo and enter clean side of shower, where fresh clothes and boots await.

Pig Containment

Since AFS is often transmitted through contact, the containment of pigs would help decrease the risk and spread of AFS. Pig containment does not allow direct or indirect pig-pig and/or pig-wild boar contacts.
